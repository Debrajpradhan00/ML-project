Detectable Conditions
🔴
Adenocarcinoma
NSCLC · MOST COMMON · ~40% OF CASES
Adenocarcinoma arises from mucus-secreting cells in the outer regions of the lung. It is the most prevalent form of non-small cell lung cancer and, uniquely, is the type most frequently found in non-smokers. It tends to grow more slowly than other subtypes and is often detected in earlier stages due to its peripheral location. CT imaging typically shows it as a ground-glass opacity or well-defined peripheral nodule, sometimes with spiculated margins.
PERIPHERAL GROUND-GLASS OPACITY NON-SMOKER EGFR MUTATIONS SLOW GROWTH
🟡
Large Cell Carcinoma
NSCLC · AGGRESSIVE · ~10–15% OF CASES
Large cell carcinoma is a heterogeneous group of undifferentiated tumours that can arise in any part of the lung. Characterised by large cells with prominent nucleoli, this type is particularly aggressive with a tendency to grow rapidly and metastasise early to regional lymph nodes and distant organs. On CT, these tumours typically appear as large peripheral masses, often with necrosis or cavitation.
RAPID GROWTH LARGE MASSES CAVITATION EARLY METASTASIS ANY LOCATION
🟣
Squamous Cell Carcinoma
NSCLC · CENTRAL · ~25–30% OF CASES
Squamous cell carcinoma originates from the flat epithelial cells lining the bronchi. It has a strong association with cigarette smoking and typically develops in the central airways. This central location often causes symptoms like haemoptysis and airway obstruction earlier than peripheral tumours. CT imaging shows it as a hilar or perihilar mass, sometimes with post-obstructive pneumonia.
SMOKING-LINKED CENTRAL AIRWAYS HAEMOPTYSIS CAVITATION SLOW GROWING
🟢
Normal
BENIGN · NO MALIGNANT INDICATORS DETECTED
When LungAI classifies a scan as Normal, the extracted texture features — including GLCM contrast, energy, correlation and homogeneity — fall within the expected statistical distribution of healthy lung tissue. No abnormal nodules, masses, or ground-glass opacities consistent with malignancy were detected in the feature space. Routine monitoring and follow-up imaging are always recommended as per standard clinical guidelines.

System Features
🌊
Gabor Texture Filters
Multi-scale, multi-orientation Gabor filters mimic the human visual cortex's edge-detection mechanism. They capture spatial frequency and orientation information critical for distinguishing malignant tissue textures from healthy parenchyma.
📐
GLCM Feature Extraction
Grey Level Co-occurrence Matrix analysis computes 14 Haralick texture descriptors — including contrast, energy, entropy, correlation, and homogeneity — that quantify spatial relationships between pixel intensities across the CT image.
🤖
RBF-Kernel SVM
A radial basis function Support Vector Machine maps the high-dimensional feature vector to a non-linear decision boundary — ideal for medical imaging tasks where class boundaries are complex and non-separable in linear space.
🎯
4-Class Multi-Output
Unlike binary screening tools, LungAI simultaneously distinguishes between Adenocarcinoma, Large Cell Carcinoma, Squamous Cell Carcinoma, and Normal tissue from a single inference call.
⚡
Sub-2s Inference
The entire pipeline — from image ingestion through feature extraction to classification — completes in under 2 seconds on standard hardware. FastAPI serves the model over a local REST endpoint for low-latency integration.
🔒
Local & Private
All computation runs locally on your machine. No CT scan data is uploaded to external servers. Patient images remain entirely within the local environment, ensuring full data privacy and HIPAA-compatible operation.

Frequently Asked Questions
What type of CT scan images does LungAI accept?▼
LungAI accepts standard 2D axial CT slice images in PNG, JPG, JPEG, BMP, and TIFF formats. The image should be a single lung cross-section slice. Full DICOM series and 3D volumetric CT data are not currently supported. For best results, use a clear, high-contrast axial slice at the level of the lesion, ideally exported from a PACS or radiology workstation at native resolution.
How does GLCM feature extraction work?▼
The Grey Level Co-occurrence Matrix (GLCM) is a statistical method that examines how often pairs of pixel intensities appear at a specific spatial relationship in an image. The model computes GLCM matrices at different angular orientations (0°, 45°, 90°, 135°) and extracts Haralick texture features including contrast, dissimilarity, homogeneity, energy, correlation, and ASM. These features describe textural patterns that differ meaningfully between healthy and malignant lung tissue.
Can LungAI detect small cell lung cancer (SCLC)?▼
No. LungAI is trained specifically on three non-small cell lung cancer (NSCLC) subtypes: Adenocarcinoma, Large Cell Carcinoma, and Squamous Cell Carcinoma. Small cell lung cancer (SCLC), mesothelioma, carcinoid tumours, and other rare pulmonary malignancies are outside the model's training distribution and may not be classified correctly. Any suspicious scan should always be reviewed by a qualified radiologist.
Is my patient data stored or sent to the cloud?▼
No. LungAI operates entirely locally. The FastAPI backend and SVM model run on your own hardware, and no image data, inference results, or metadata are transmitted to any external server or cloud service. The browser communicates only with the local server at 127.0.0.1:8000. Ensure all scans are properly anonymised before uploading.
What does the confidence percentage represent?▼
The confidence percentage represents the SVM model's probability estimate for the predicted class, derived from Platt scaling. A score above 80% indicates high certainty. Scores between 55–80% are moderate — treat with caution. Scores below 55% are flagged as "Uncertain," typically indicating a low-quality input image or a case near a decision boundary in feature space. Radiologist review is essential in all uncertain cases.
How do I run the backend server?▼
After installing dependencies (pip install fastapi uvicorn scikit-learn scikit-image pillow numpy joblib), run: uvicorn server:app --host 127.0.0.1 --port 8000. Ensure your trained model file is in the expected path referenced by server.py. The frontend will automatically detect the server status and enable the Analyze button once a successful health check is received from the / endpoint.
Limitations & Responsible Use
⚠️
Known Technical Limitations
Accuracy is dependent on input image quality. Blurry or low-contrast CT images may significantly reduce classification reliability.
Only single 2D axial slice images are processed — 3D volumetric CT series and multi-planar reconstructions are not currently supported.
Rare subtypes such as mesothelioma, carcinoid tumours, or SCLC are outside the training distribution and may be incorrectly classified.
Very small nodules under ~5mm may not produce sufficient texture variation for reliable GLCM-based classification.
Model performance has not been prospectively validated across diverse patient populations, scanner manufacturers, or varied imaging protocols.
⚕️
Medical & Ethical Guidelines
This system is for research and educational purposes only. It must not be the sole basis for any clinical diagnosis or treatment decision.
All AI outputs should be reviewed by a qualified radiologist or oncologist before any clinical action is taken.
The system does not store or transmit patient-identifiable information. All scans should be fully anonymised before upload.
A "Normal" classification does not guarantee absence of malignancy — routine follow-up imaging is always recommended.
Developers and institutions deploying this tool bear full responsibility for compliance with applicable medical device regulations.
